Technical Analysis Patterns

Master chart patterns to identify trading opportunities and market trends

Bullish Patterns

Patterns indicating potential upward price movement

Inverse Head and Shoulders

Bullish Reversal
Reliability: High

A bullish reversal pattern that forms after a downtrend. It consists of three troughs with the middle trough (head) being the deepest, and two shallower troughs (shoulders) on either side.

Double Bottom

Bullish Reversal
Reliability: High

A bullish reversal pattern characterized by two consecutive troughs at approximately the same price level, resembling the letter "W".

Cup and Handle

Bullish Continuation
Reliability: High

A bullish continuation pattern that resembles a teacup. The cup is a rounded bottom, and the handle is a slight downward drift after the cup formation.

Ascending Triangle

Bullish Continuation
Reliability: Medium-High

A bullish continuation pattern with a flat upper resistance line and rising lower support line, indicating accumulation.

Bullish Flag

Bullish Continuation
Reliability: Medium

A short-term continuation pattern that resembles a flag on a pole. Forms after a strong upward move (flagpole) followed by a consolidation (flag).

Morning Star

Bullish Reversal
Reliability: High

A three-candle bullish reversal pattern that appears at the bottom of a downtrend, signaling a potential trend reversal.

Bearish Patterns

Patterns indicating potential downward price movement

Head and Shoulders

Bearish Reversal
Reliability: High

A bearish reversal pattern that forms after an uptrend. It consists of three peaks with the middle peak (head) being the highest, and two lower peaks (shoulders) on either side.

Double Top

Bearish Reversal
Reliability: High

A bearish reversal pattern characterized by two consecutive peaks at approximately the same price level, resembling the letter "M".

Descending Triangle

Bearish Continuation
Reliability: Medium-High

A bearish continuation pattern with a flat lower support line and descending upper resistance line, indicating distribution.

Bearish Flag

Bearish Continuation
Reliability: Medium

A short-term continuation pattern that forms after a strong downward move (flagpole) followed by a consolidation (flag).

Evening Star

Bearish Reversal
Reliability: High

A three-candle bearish reversal pattern that appears at the top of an uptrend, signaling a potential trend reversal.

Rising Wedge

Bearish Reversal
Reliability: Medium

A bearish pattern where both support and resistance lines slope upward, but converge, indicating weakening momentum.

Neutral Patterns

Patterns indicating consolidation before continuation

Symmetrical Triangle

Neutral Continuation
Reliability: Medium

A continuation pattern where price converges between a descending resistance line and an ascending support line, indicating consolidation.

Rectangle

Neutral Continuation
Reliability: Medium

A consolidation pattern with horizontal support and resistance lines, indicating a trading range before continuation.

Pennant

Neutral Continuation
Reliability: Medium

A short-term continuation pattern that forms after a strong move, characterized by converging trendlines forming a small symmetrical triangle.
